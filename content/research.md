## Articles

**Investment Games**
_Duke Law Journal_ (forthcoming 2022)
A systematic examination of "gamification" in stock trading apps â€” assessing how we got here, situating gamification within securities law's investor protection concerns, offering a social welfare case for regulation, and canvassing doctrinal and normative implications.
[SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3916407)

**On Confetti Regulation: The Wrong Way to Regulate Gamified Investing**
(with Kyle Langvardt), 131 _Yale Law Journal Forum_ (2021)
The first piece of legal scholarship to describe gamified investing as a regulatory phenomenon, discussing the litigation risk associated with banning undesirable app design features.
[SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3928268)

**Contracts' Framing Effects on Legal Reform**
98 _Nebraska Law Review_ (2020)
An examination of how companies use high-quality consumer contract terms not for courts or consumers, but to frame the status quo for policymakers deciding whether to prohibit other, more important contract terms.

## Comment Letters

SEC File No. S7-10-21 (2021)
Comments on the SEC's request for information on digital engagement practices in stock trading apps, summarizing my scholarship for regulators and recommending possible interventions.

**Amendments to Exchange Act Rule 3b-16 (Reg ATS Reform)**
SEC File No. S7-02-22 (2022)
Comments highlighting litigation risks to the SEC's proposed amendments to Regulation ATS, and inviting a concept release on extending Reg ATS to communication protocols in cryptocurrency and decentralized finance markets.
